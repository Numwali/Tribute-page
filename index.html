Marie Curie

The first woman to win a Nobel Prize and a pioneer in radioactivity research

Marie Curie, circa 1920, in her lab.

Here's a timeline of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 – Born in Warsaw, Poland, as Maria Sklodowska
  • 1891 – Moves to Paris to study at the Sorbonne
  • 1895 – Marries Pierre Curie, her collaborator and fellow physicist
  • 1898 – Discovers the elements polonium and radium
  • 1903 – Becomes the first woman to win a Nobel Prize (Physics)
  • 1911 – Wins a second Nobel Prize (Chemistry) for her work on radioactivity
  • 1934 – Dies from aplastic anemia, caused by prolonged exposure to radiation
"One never notices what has been done; one can only see what remains to be done."
– Marie Curie
